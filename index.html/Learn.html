Climate Change

Learn about the global climate crisis, its causes, impacts, and how we can work together to create a sustainable future.

Information About Climate Change

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns, primarily driven by human activities, especially the burning of fossil fuels.

What Is Climate Change?

Climate change is the long-term alteration of temperature and typical weather patterns in a place. The cause of current climate change is largely human activity, like burning fossil fuels, which adds heat-trapping gases to Earth's atmosphere.

The term "climate change" represents a change in the average conditions — such as temperature and rainfall — in a region over a long period of time. Scientists have observed that Earth's temperature is rising at an unprecedented rate over the past century.

Some Causes of Climate Change

Understanding the primary drivers behind global climate change with brief definitions below.

Fossil Fuels

Burning coal, oil, and natural gas releases greenhouse gases into the atmosphere, trapping heat and warming the planet.

Deforestation

Cutting down forests reduces the planet's ability to absorb carbon dioxide while releasing stored carbon back into the atmosphere.

Agriculture & Livestock

Modern farming practices and livestock production contribute significant amounts of methane and nitrous oxide, both potent greenhouse gases.

Impacts of Climate Change

How climate change affects our planet, communities, and daily lives.

Rising Temperatures and Extreme Weather conditions

Global warming leads to more frequent and intense heat waves, droughts, and heavy precipitation events. These extremes threaten human health, agriculture, and infrastructure.

Since 1980, the number of extreme weather events has increased significantly, causing hundreds of billions in damages and affecting millions of people worldwide.

Sea Level Rise and Ocean Acidification

Melting ice sheets and glaciers contribute to rising sea levels, threatening coastal communities and ecosystems. Meanwhile, oceans absorb about 30% of atmospheric CO₂, making them more acidic and endangering marine life.

Projections suggest sea levels could rise by 1-8 feet by 2100, potentially displacing hundreds of millions of people.

Climate Solutions

Exploring the strategies and technologies that can help us address the climate crisis.

Renewable Energy

Solar, wind, and hydroelectric power offer clean alternatives to fossil fuels, reducing emissions while creating jobs and improving energy security.

Sustainable Agriculture

Regenerative farming practices can reduce emissions, sequester carbon in soil,
and build resilience to changing climate conditions.

Policy & Innovation

Carbon pricing, emissions regulations, and investments in green technology
can drive the transition to a low-carbon economy.
